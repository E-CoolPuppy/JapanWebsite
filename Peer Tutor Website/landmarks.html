Mount Fuji, Shizuoka (富士山)

At 3,778 meters high, Mount Fuji is the highest mountain in Japan. It is considered an active volcano,
although it has not erupted since 1707. A symbol of Japanese culture, it has been recreated in art and
literature throughout history. Today, people continue to revere Mount Fuji, whether through the shrines
that surround it or climbing to the top, which is believed to be “purification” since hundreds of years ago.
All in all, along with the Fuji Five Lakes that surround it, Mount Fuji is an amazing sight to see for
everyone.

Fushimi Inari Shrine, Kyoto (伏見稲荷大社)

Located in southern Kyoto, the Fushimi Inari Shrine is one of the most significant
Shinto shrines. It is made up of thousands of tori gates, which leads to a trail that
goes into the massive forest behind the shrine. Inside the forest lies Mount Inari,
a 233 meter-high mountain. Throughout the shrine are numerous fox statues, as
the shrine is dedicated to Inari, the Shinto god of rice. Additionally, at the shrine’s
entrance is the Romon gate, which was donated by Toyotomi Hideyoshi in 1589,
and still stands today.

Aokigahara / Suicide Forest, Yamanashi (青木ヶ原)

Since the 1950s, people have been wandering into the forest, known as “the perfect place
to die”. In 2003, a catastrophic number of 105 bodies were discovered. Today, signs can
be seen throughout the area, pleading any that enter with suicidal thoughts to reconsider.
In hopes of not attracting any more potential suicides, Japanese authorities have stopped
publishing the exact numbers of those discovered in the forest.

Tokyo Tower, Tokyo (東京タワー)

One of the most famous landmarks of Tokyo is Tokyo Tower, a 333 meter-high
tower that stands as the world’s tallest, self-supported steel tower, even besting
the Eiffel Tower by 13 meters! It was built in 1958 to represent Japan’s economic
success and revival after surviving war. It also serves as a broadcast antenna for
the city. Although the completion of the Tokyo Skytree in 2012 bested the tower’s
height at a staggering 634 meters, the tower remains an important landmark
today.
